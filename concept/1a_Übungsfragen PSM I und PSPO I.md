# Übungsfragen zur Vorbereitung: PSM I & PSPO I

Diese Sammlung enthält **je 50 Übungsfragen** für die Zertifizierungen **Professional Scrum Master™ I (PSM I)** und **Professional Scrum Product Owner™ I (PSPO I)**. Die Fragen sind **auf Englisch** formuliert – wie die echte Scrum.org-Prüfung – und am **Scrum Guide 2020** ausgerichtet.

> **Wichtig:** Dies sind **eigenständig erstellte Übungsfragen im Stil** der Scrum.org-Assessments – **keine** Originalfragen der Prüfung. Sie dienen dem Üben von Verständnis, Terminologie und Tempo. Maßgeblich ist immer der offizielle Scrum Guide.

**So nutzen Sie die Fragen:**

- Beantworten Sie zunächst einen Block, **ohne** in den Lösungsteil zu schauen.
- Simulieren Sie den Zeitdruck der echten Prüfung: ~**45 Sekunden pro Frage**.
- Achten Sie auf Fragen mit dem Hinweis **„(Choose all that apply.)"** – hier sind mehrere Antworten korrekt, und nur die vollständig richtige Auswahl zählt.
- Vergleichen Sie danach mit dem **Lösungsschlüssel** und lesen Sie die **Begründung** – Ziel ist Verständnis, nicht Auswendiglernen.

## PSM I — Answer Key & Explanations

**1. B** — The Scrum Guide states a Scrum Team is typically 10 or fewer people; smaller teams communicate better and are more productive.

**2. B** — A Scrum Team comprises one Scrum Master, one Product Owner, and Developers. There are no sub-teams or hierarchies.

**3. C** — The Product Owner is accountable for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Scrum Team.

**4. B** — Empiricism rests on three pillars: Transparency, Inspection, and Adaptation.

**5. A** — The five Scrum Values are Commitment, Courage, Focus, Openness, and Respect.

**6. C** — Only the Product Owner has the authority to cancel a Sprint (e.g., if the Sprint Goal becomes obsolete).

**7. B** — Sprints are fixed-length events of one month or less.

**8. A (True)** — A new Sprint starts immediately after the conclusion of the previous one.

**9. A** — The Daily Scrum is a 15-minute event for the Developers.

**10. C** — The Daily Scrum is for the Developers to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the Sprint Backlog.

**11. B** — The Sprint is a container for all other events (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective).

**12. A** — The Sprint Review inspects the outcome of the Sprint (the Increment) and determines future adaptations to the Product Backlog. It is a working session, not a sign-off gate.

**13. B** — The Retrospective inspects how the last Sprint went regarding individuals, interactions, processes, tools, and the Definition of Done, and identifies improvements.

**14. B** — Sprint Planning is time-boxed to a maximum of 8 hours for a one-month Sprint (usually shorter for shorter Sprints).

**15. A, B, C** — Sprint Planning addresses Why (the Sprint Goal/value), What (which items), and How (the plan). Tasks are not assigned by the Scrum Master; the Developers self-organize.

**16. B** — The Sprint Goal is the commitment for the Sprint Backlog.

**17. C** — The Product Goal is the commitment for the Product Backlog.

**18. C** — The Definition of Done is the commitment for the Increment.

**19. C** — If the Definition of Done is not an organizational standard, the Scrum Team must create one appropriate for the product.

**20. B (False)** — If an item does not meet the Definition of Done it cannot be released or even presented at the Sprint Review; it returns to the Product Backlog.

**21. C** — The Sprint Backlog is created by and belongs to the Developers.

**22. B** — The Scrum Master is accountable for the Scrum Team's effectiveness by helping it improve its practices.

**23. A, B, C** — The Scrum Master serves the Scrum Team, the Product Owner, and the wider organization.

**24. B** — Self-managing means the Scrum Team internally decides who does what, when, and how.

**25. B** — Scrum is founded on empiricism and lean thinking.

**26. B (False)** — The Scrum Master is a true leader who serves; the role has no command authority over the Developers.

**27. B** — Only enough detail is needed to start; the Product Backlog is emergent and continuously refined.

**28. B** — Refinement is the ongoing act of breaking down and further defining items so they are precise enough to be worked on.

**29. C** — The Product Owner orders (prioritizes) the Product Backlog.

**30. A (True)** — Scope may be clarified and renegotiated with the Product Owner as more is learned, as long as the Sprint Goal remains.

**31. B** — The Developers negotiate scope with the Product Owner. The Sprint length is fixed and the Sprint is not extended.

**32. B** — An Increment is a concrete, usable stepping stone toward the Product Goal.

**33. B** — Multiple Increments may be created within a single Sprint.

**34. B (False)** — The Sprint Review is a working session to inspect and adapt, not just a status presentation.

**35. B** — The Sprint Retrospective is time-boxed to a maximum of 3 hours for a one-month Sprint.

**36. C** — The Sprint Review is time-boxed to a maximum of 4 hours for a one-month Sprint.

**37. B** — For the Product Owner to succeed, the entire organization must respect their decisions; no one can force the Developers to work from a different set of requirements.

**38. B** — The Scrum Master coaches the team to keep the Daily Scrum within its 15-minute time-box; follow-up discussions can happen afterward.

**39. B (False)** — Scrum is a lightweight, intentionally incomplete framework; it does not prescribe detailed techniques.

**40. A** — The Scrum Master helps the team focus on the Sprint Goal and coaches the organization. The Sprint Goal stays fixed, and only the Product Owner can adjust product scope.

**41. A, B, D** — The Sprint Goal is the single objective, crafted in Sprint Planning, and gives flexibility on the exact work. It is not abandoned freely mid-Sprint.

**42. B** — Cross-functional means the team collectively has all skills needed to create value each Sprint without depending on others.

**43. A (True)** — The Product Owner may delegate but remains accountable for Product Backlog management.

**44. B** — An Increment is "Done" when it meets the Definition of Done.

**45. B** — The Scrum Master coaches the organization to channel product decisions through the Product Owner and protects the team's focus.

**46. B (False)** — The Sprint Backlog is updated throughout the Sprint as the Developers learn more; it is a real-time picture of the plan.

**47. B** — The Sprint creates consistency: a fixed-length period that yields a usable Increment toward the Product Goal.

**48. B** — The Sprint Review is attended by the Scrum Team and key stakeholders invited by the Product Owner.

**49. B** — A Definition of Done is essential for transparency; if none exists, the Scrum Team must create one.

**50. A (True)** — Scrum 2020 uses "accountabilities" rather than "roles," and the Developers are simply members of the one Scrum Team (no separate Development Team).
